### Solved Exercises 11.2 (Questions 9&10)

**9. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-\pi, \pi)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
0, & -\pi < x < 0 \\
\sin x, & 0 \le x < \pi 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = \pi$. The terms are of the form $\sin nx$ and $\cos nx$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \, dx = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin x \, dx
$$
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} [-\cos x]_0^{\pi} = \frac{1}{\pi} (-\cos \pi + \cos 0) = \frac{1}{\pi} (-(-1) + 1) = \frac{2}{\pi}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin x \cos nx \, dx
$$
Using the trigonometric identity $\sin A \cos B = \frac{1}{2}[\sin(A+B) + \sin(A-B)]$:
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} [\sin((1+n)x) + \sin((1-n)x)] \, dx
$$
**Case 1: $n=1$.**
$$
a_1 = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} (\sin 2x + 0) \, dx = \frac{1}{2\pi} [-\frac{1}{2}\cos 2x]_0^{\pi} = -\frac{1}{4\pi}(1 - 1) = 0
$$
**Case 2: $n > 1$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi} \left[ -\frac{\cos(1+n)x}{1+n} - \frac{\cos(1-n)x}{1-n} \right]_0^{\pi}
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi} \left[ \left( -\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n+1} - \frac{(-1)^{1-n}}{1-n} \right) - \left( -\frac{1}{n+1} - \frac{1}{1-n} \right) \right]
$$
Simplifying using $(-1)^{n+1} = -(-1)^n$ and $1-n = -(n-1)$:
$$
a_n = \frac{1 + (-1)^n}{\pi(1 - n^2)}
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin x \sin nx \, dx
$$
Using $\sin A \sin B = \frac{1}{2}[\cos(A-B) - \cos(A+B)]$:
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} [\cos(1-n)x - \cos(1+n)x] \, dx
$$
**Case 1: $n=1$.**
$$
b_1 = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} (1 - \cos 2x) \, dx = \frac{1}{2\pi} [x - \frac{1}{2}\sin 2x]_0^{\pi} = \frac{1}{2\pi}(\pi) = \frac{1}{2}
$$
**Case 2: $n > 1$.**
The integral of $\cos kx$ over $[0, \pi]$ where $k$ is an integer $\neq 0$ involves $\sin k\pi$, which is 0.
$$
b_n = 0 \quad \text{for } n > 1
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{\pi} + \frac{1}{2}\sin x + \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} \frac{1 + (-1)^n}{\pi(1 - n^2)} \cos nx
$$

**10. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-\pi/2, \pi/2)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
0, & -\pi/2 < x < 0 \\
\cos x, & 0 \le x < \pi/2 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here the interval length is $\pi$, so $2p = \pi \implies p = \pi/2$.
The basis functions are $\cos(\frac{n\pi x}{\pi/2}) = \cos 2nx$ and $\sin 2nx$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi/2} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \cos x \, dx = \frac{2}{\pi} [\sin x]_0^{\pi/2} = \frac{2}{\pi}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \cos x \cos 2nx \, dx
$$
Using $\cos A \cos B = \frac{1}{2}[\cos(A+B) + \cos(A-B)]$:
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} [\cos(2n+1)x + \cos(1-2n)x] \, dx
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \frac{\sin(2n+1)x}{2n+1} + \frac{\sin(1-2n)x}{1-2n} \right]_0^{\pi/2}
$$
Note that $\sin((2n+1)\frac{\pi}{2}) = \sin(n\pi + \frac{\pi}{2}) = (-1)^n$. Similarly $\sin((1-2n)\frac{\pi}{2}) = (-1)^n$.
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \frac{(-1)^n}{2n+1} + \frac{(-1)^n}{1-2n} \right] = \frac{(-1)^n}{\pi} \left( \frac{1}{2n+1} - \frac{1}{2n-1} \right)
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{(-1)^n}{\pi} \left( \frac{2n-1 - (2n+1)}{4n^2-1} \right) = \frac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{\pi(4n^2-1)}
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \cos x \sin 2nx \, dx
$$
Using $\sin A \cos B = \frac{1}{2}[\sin(A+B) + \sin(A-B)]$:
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} [\sin(2n+1)x + \sin(2n-1)x] \, dx
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ -\frac{\cos(2n+1)x}{2n+1} - \frac{\cos(2n-1)x}{2n-1} \right]_0^{\pi/2}
$$
Since $\cos$ of odd multiples of $\pi/2$ is 0, the upper limit evaluation is 0.
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ 0 - \left( -\frac{1}{2n+1} - \frac{1}{2n-1} \right) \right] = \frac{1}{\pi} \left( \frac{2n-1 + 2n+1}{4n^2-1} \right) = \frac{4n}{\pi(4n^2-1)}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{\pi} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{\pi(4n^2-1)} \cos 2nx + \frac{4n}{\pi(4n^2-1)} \sin 2nx \right]
$$
