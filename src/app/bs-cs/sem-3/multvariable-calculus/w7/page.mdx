### General Formulas

For a function $f(x)$ defined on the interval $(-p, p)$, the Fourier series is given by:

$$
f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( a_n \cos \frac{n\pi}{p}x + b_n \sin \frac{n\pi}{p}x \right)
$$

The coefficients are calculated using the following integrals:

$$
\begin{aligned}
a_0 &= \frac{1}{p} \int_{-p}^{p} f(x) \, dx \\
a_n &= \frac{1}{p} \int_{-p}^{p} f(x) \cos \frac{n\pi}{p}x \, dx \\
b_n &= \frac{1}{p} \int_{-p}^{p} f(x) \sin \frac{n\pi}{p}x \, dx
\end{aligned}
$$

### Solved Exercises 11.2 (Questions 1â€“8)

**1. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-\pi, \pi)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
0, & -\pi < x < 0 \\
1, & 0 \le x < \pi 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here, the interval is $(-\pi, \pi)$, so $p = \pi$. The terms $\frac{n\pi}{p}x$ simplify to $nx$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \, dx = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \int_{-\pi}^{0} 0 \, dx + \int_{0}^{\pi} 1 \, dx \right]
$$
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} [x]_0^{\pi} = \frac{1}{\pi}(\pi - 0) = 1
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \cos nx \, dx = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} 1 \cdot \cos nx \, dx
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \frac{\sin nx}{n} \right]_0^{\pi} = \frac{1}{n\pi} (\sin n\pi - \sin 0) = 0
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \sin nx \, dx = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} 1 \cdot \sin nx \, dx
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ -\frac{\cos nx}{n} \right]_0^{\pi} = -\frac{1}{n\pi} (\cos n\pi - \cos 0)
$$
Since $\cos n\pi = (-1)^n$ and $\cos 0 = 1$:
$$
b_n = \frac{1 - (-1)^n}{n\pi}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1 - (-1)^n}{n\pi} \sin nx
$$

**2. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-\pi, \pi)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
-1, & -\pi < x < 0 \\
2, & 0 \le x < \pi 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = \pi$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \int_{-\pi}^{0} -1 \, dx + \int_{0}^{\pi} 2 \, dx \right]
$$
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \left( [-x]_{-\pi}^{0} + [2x]_{0}^{\pi} \right) = \frac{1}{\pi} ( (0 - \pi) + (2\pi - 0) ) = \frac{\pi}{\pi} = 1
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \int_{-\pi}^{0} -\cos nx \, dx + \int_{0}^{\pi} 2 \cos nx \, dx \right]
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left( \left[ -\frac{\sin nx}{n} \right]_{-\pi}^{0} + \left[ \frac{2\sin nx}{n} \right]_{0}^{\pi} \right)
$$
Since $\sin(0) = \sin(n\pi) = \sin(-n\pi) = 0$, all terms vanish.
$$
a_n = 0
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \int_{-\pi}^{0} -\sin nx \, dx + \int_{0}^{\pi} 2 \sin nx \, dx \right]
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left( \left[ \frac{\cos nx}{n} \right]_{-\pi}^{0} + \left[ -\frac{2\cos nx}{n} \right]_{0}^{\pi} \right)
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{n\pi} \left( (\cos 0 - \cos(-n\pi)) - 2(\cos n\pi - \cos 0) \right)
$$
Using $\cos(-n\pi) = \cos n\pi = (-1)^n$ and $\cos 0 = 1$:
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{n\pi} \left( (1 - (-1)^n) - 2((-1)^n - 1) \right)
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{n\pi} ( 1 - (-1)^n - 2(-1)^n + 2 ) = \frac{3 - 3(-1)^n}{n\pi}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{3(1 - (-1)^n)}{n\pi} \sin nx
$$

**3. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-1, 1)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
1, & -1 < x < 0 \\
x, & 0 \le x < 1 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = 1$. The argument for sine and cosine becomes $\frac{n\pi}{1}x = n\pi x$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{1} \left[ \int_{-1}^{0} 1 \, dx + \int_{0}^{1} x \, dx \right]
$$
$$
a_0 = [x]_{-1}^{0} + \left[\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_{0}^{1} = (0 - (-1)) + (\frac{1}{2} - 0) = 1 + 0.5 = \frac{3}{2}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \int_{-1}^{0} \cos n\pi x \, dx + \int_{0}^{1} x \cos n\pi x \, dx
$$
The first integral:
$$
\left[ \frac{\sin n\pi x}{n\pi} \right]_{-1}^{0} = 0
$$
The second integral (Integration by Parts: $u=x, dv=\cos n\pi x dx$):
$$
\int_{0}^{1} x \cos n\pi x \, dx = \left[ \frac{x \sin n\pi x}{n\pi} \right]_0^1 - \frac{1}{n\pi} \int_0^1 \sin n\pi x \, dx
$$
$$
= (0 - 0) - \frac{1}{n\pi} \left[ -\frac{\cos n\pi x}{n\pi} \right]_0^1 = \frac{1}{n^2\pi^2} (\cos n\pi - \cos 0)
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{(-1)^n - 1}{n^2\pi^2}
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \int_{-1}^{0} \sin n\pi x \, dx + \int_{0}^{1} x \sin n\pi x \, dx
$$
The first integral:
$$
\left[ -\frac{\cos n\pi x}{n\pi} \right]_{-1}^{0} = -\frac{1}{n\pi} (1 - \cos(-n\pi)) = \frac{(-1)^n - 1}{n\pi}
$$
The second integral (Integration by Parts: $u=x, dv=\sin n\pi x dx$):
$$
\int_{0}^{1} x \sin n\pi x \, dx = \left[ -\frac{x \cos n\pi x}{n\pi} \right]_0^1 + \frac{1}{n\pi} \int_0^1 \cos n\pi x \, dx
$$
$$
= \left( -\frac{1 \cdot (-1)^n}{n\pi} - 0 \right) + \frac{1}{n^2\pi^2} [\sin n\pi x]_0^1 = -\frac{(-1)^n}{n\pi}
$$
Adding the two parts:
$$
b_n = \frac{(-1)^n - 1}{n\pi} - \frac{(-1)^n}{n\pi} = -\frac{1}{n\pi}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{3}{4} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( \frac{(-1)^n - 1}{n^2\pi^2} \cos n\pi x - \frac{1}{n\pi} \sin n\pi x \right)
$$

**4. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-1, 1)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
0, & -1 < x < 0 \\
x, & 0 \le x < 1 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = 1$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \int_{0}^{1} x \, dx = \left[\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_0^1 = \frac{1}{2}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \int_{0}^{1} x \cos n\pi x \, dx
$$
Using the integration by parts result from Question 3:
$$
a_n = \frac{(-1)^n - 1}{n^2\pi^2}
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \int_{0}^{1} x \sin n\pi x \, dx
$$
Using the integration by parts result from Question 3:
$$
b_n = -\frac{(-1)^n}{n\pi} = \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n\pi}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{4} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( \frac{(-1)^n - 1}{n^2\pi^2} \cos n\pi x + \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n\pi} \sin n\pi x \right)
$$

**5. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-\pi, \pi)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
0, & -\pi < x < 0 \\
x^2, & 0 \le x < \pi 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = \pi$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} x^2 \, dx = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^{\pi} = \frac{\pi^2}{3}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} x^2 \cos nx \, dx
$$
Use integration by parts ($u=x^2, dv=\cos nx dx$) then ($u=2x, dv=\sin nx dx$):
$$
\int x^2 \cos nx \, dx = \frac{x^2 \sin nx}{n} + \frac{2x \cos nx}{n^2} - \frac{2 \sin nx}{n^3}
$$
Evaluate from $0$ to $\pi$:
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \left(0 + \frac{2\pi \cos n\pi}{n^2} - 0\right) - (0 + 0 - 0) \right]
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left( \frac{2\pi (-1)^n}{n^2} \right) = \frac{2(-1)^n}{n^2}
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} x^2 \sin nx \, dx
$$
Use integration by parts ($u=x^2, dv=\sin nx dx$) then ($u=2x, dv=\cos nx dx$):
$$
\int x^2 \sin nx \, dx = -\frac{x^2 \cos nx}{n} + \frac{2x \sin nx}{n^2} + \frac{2 \cos nx}{n^3}
$$
Evaluate from $0$ to $\pi$:
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left( \left[ -\frac{\pi^2 (-1)^n}{n} + 0 + \frac{2(-1)^n}{n^3} \right] - \left[ 0 + 0 + \frac{2}{n^3} \right] \right)
$$
$$
b_n = -\frac{\pi (-1)^n}{n} + \frac{2((-1)^n - 1)}{\pi n^3} = \frac{\pi (-1)^{n+1}}{n} + \frac{2((-1)^n - 1)}{\pi n^3}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{\pi^2}{6} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( \frac{2(-1)^n}{n^2} \cos nx + \left[ \frac{\pi (-1)^{n+1}}{n} + \frac{2((-1)^n - 1)}{\pi n^3} \right] \sin nx \right)
$$

**6. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-\pi, \pi)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
\pi^2, & -\pi < x < 0 \\
\pi^2 - x^2, & 0 \le x < \pi 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = \pi$.
Notice that $f(x) = \pi^2 - g(x)$, where $g(x)$ is the function from Question 5 (extended to be 0 on negative side).
The Fourier series of the constant $\pi^2$ on $(-\pi, \pi)$ has only the $a_0$ term: $2\pi^2$ (since formula is $a_0/2$).
However, calculating directly:

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \int_{-\pi}^{0} \pi^2 \, dx + \int_{0}^{\pi} (\pi^2 - x^2) \, dx \right]
$$
$$
= \frac{1}{\pi} \left( [\pi^2 x]_{-\pi}^0 + [\pi^2 x - \frac{x^3}{3}]_0^{\pi} \right)
$$
$$
= \frac{1}{\pi} \left( \pi^3 + (\pi^3 - \frac{\pi^3}{3}) \right) = \frac{1}{\pi} \left( \frac{5\pi^3}{3} \right) = \frac{5\pi^2}{3}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \int_{-\pi}^{0} \pi^2 \cos nx \, dx + \int_{0}^{\pi} (\pi^2 - x^2) \cos nx \, dx \right]
$$
The integral of the constant $\pi^2 \cos nx$ over any multiple of the period (or half period) is 0.
Thus, $a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} -x^2 \cos nx \, dx$.
This is the negative of the $a_n$ from Question 5:
$$
a_n = -\frac{2(-1)^n}{n^2} = \frac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{n^2}
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \int_{-\pi}^{0} \pi^2 \sin nx \, dx + \int_{0}^{\pi} (\pi^2 - x^2) \sin nx \, dx \right]
$$
Integral 1: $\pi^2 [-\frac{\cos nx}{n}]_{-\pi}^0 = -\frac{\pi^2}{n}(1 - (-1)^n)$.
Integral 2: $\pi^2 \int \sin - \int x^2 \sin$.
$\int_0^{\pi} \pi^2 \sin nx dx = \frac{\pi^2}{n}(1 - (-1)^n)$.
The constant terms cancel out: $-\frac{\pi^2}{n}(1 - (-1)^n) + \frac{\pi^2}{n}(1 - (-1)^n) = 0$.
Remaining term: $-\frac{1}{\pi} \int_0^{\pi} x^2 \sin nx \, dx$.
This is the negative of the $b_n$ from Question 5.
$$
b_n = - \left[ \frac{\pi (-1)^{n+1}}{n} + \frac{2((-1)^n - 1)}{\pi n^3} \right]
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{5\pi^2}{6} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( \frac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{n^2} \cos nx - \left[ \frac{\pi (-1)^{n+1}}{n} + \frac{2((-1)^n - 1)}{\pi n^3} \right] \sin nx \right)
$$

**7. Find the Fourier series of $f(x) = x + \pi$ on $(-\pi, \pi)$.**

**Solution:**
Here $p = \pi$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (x + \pi) \, dx = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \frac{x^2}{2} + \pi x \right]_{-\pi}^{\pi}
$$
$$
= \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ (\frac{\pi^2}{2} + \pi^2) - (\frac{\pi^2}{2} - \pi^2) \right] = \frac{1}{\pi} (2\pi^2) = 2\pi
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (x + \pi) \cos nx \, dx
$$
Split into $\int x \cos nx$ and $\pi \int \cos nx$.
$x \cos nx$ is an odd function over a symmetric interval, so the integral is 0.
$\cos nx$ over a full period is 0.
$$
a_n = 0
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (x + \pi) \sin nx \, dx
$$
$\pi \sin nx$ is odd over symmetric interval $\to 0$.
$x \sin nx$ is even over symmetric interval $\to 2 \int_0^{\pi} x \sin nx$.
$$
b_n = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} x \sin nx \, dx
$$
Integration by parts: $[-\frac{x \cos nx}{n}]_0^{\pi} + \int \frac{\cos nx}{n} dx$.
$$
b_n = \frac{2}{\pi} \left[ -\frac{\pi (-1)^n}{n} - 0 \right] = -\frac{2(-1)^n}{n} = \frac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{n}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \pi + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{n} \sin nx
$$

**8. Find the Fourier series of $f(x) = 3 - 2x$ on $(-\pi, \pi)$.**

**Solution:**
Here $p = \pi$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (3 - 2x) \, dx = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ 3x - x^2 \right]_{-\pi}^{\pi}
$$
$$
= \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ (3\pi - \pi^2) - (-3\pi - \pi^2) \right] = \frac{1}{\pi} (6\pi) = 6
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (3 - 2x) \cos nx \, dx
$$
$3 \cos nx$ integrates to 0.
$-2x \cos nx$ is odd, integrates to 0.
$$
a_n = 0
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (3 - 2x) \sin nx \, dx
$$
$3 \sin nx$ is odd, integrates to 0.
$-2x \sin nx$ is even.
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} -2x \sin nx \, dx = -\frac{4}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} x \sin nx \, dx
$$
Using the integration result from Question 7 ($\int_0^{\pi} x \sin nx dx = -\frac{\pi (-1)^n}{n}$):
$$
b_n = -\frac{4}{\pi} \left( -\frac{\pi (-1)^n}{n} \right) = \frac{4(-1)^n}{n}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = 3 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{4(-1)^n}{n} \sin nx
$$
