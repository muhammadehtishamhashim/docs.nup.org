### Solved Examples (from the Book)

**11. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-2, 2)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
0, & -2 < x < -1 \\
-2, & -1 \le x < 0 \\
1, & 0 \le x < 1 \\
0, & 1 \le x < 2 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = 2$. Basis functions: $\cos \frac{n\pi x}{2}, \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2}$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \int_{-1}^{0} -2 \, dx + \int_{0}^{1} 1 \, dx \right]
$$
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{2} ( -2(1) + 1(1) ) = -\frac{1}{2}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \int_{-1}^{0} -2 \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx + \int_{0}^{1} 1 \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx \right]
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2} \left[ -2 \frac{2}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \Big|_{-1}^0 + \frac{2}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \Big|_0^1 \right]
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{n\pi} \left[ -2(0 - \sin \frac{-n\pi}{2}) + (\sin \frac{n\pi}{2} - 0) \right]
$$
Since $\sin(-A) = -\sin A$:
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{n\pi} (-2\sin \frac{n\pi}{2} + \sin \frac{n\pi}{2}) = -\frac{1}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2}
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \int_{-1}^{0} -2 \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx + \int_{0}^{1} 1 \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx \right]
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{2} \left[ -2 \frac{-2}{n\pi} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \Big|_{-1}^0 + \frac{-2}{n\pi} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \Big|_0^1 \right]
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{n\pi} \left[ 2(1 - \cos \frac{-n\pi}{2}) - (\cos \frac{n\pi}{2} - 1) \right]
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{n\pi} (2 - 2\cos \frac{n\pi}{2} - \cos \frac{n\pi}{2} + 1) = \frac{3}{n\pi} \left( 1 - \cos \frac{n\pi}{2} \right)
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = -\frac{1}{4} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ -\frac{1}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} + \frac{3}{n\pi} \left( 1 - \cos \frac{n\pi}{2} \right) \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \right]
$$

**12. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-2, 2)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
0, & -2 < x < 0 \\
x, & 0 \le x < 1 \\
1, & 1 \le x < 2 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = 2$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \int_{0}^{1} x \, dx + \int_{1}^{2} 1 \, dx \right]
$$
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{2} \left( [\frac{x^2}{2}]_0^1 + [x]_1^2 \right) = \frac{1}{2} (\frac{1}{2} + 1) = \frac{3}{4}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \int_{0}^{1} x \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx + \int_{1}^{2} 1 \cdot \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx \right]
$$
Integration by parts for the first integral ($u=x, dv=\cos \dots$):
$$
\int x \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} dx = \frac{2x}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} + \frac{4}{n^2\pi^2} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2}
$$
Evaluate limits for first part ($0$ to $1$):
$$
\frac{2}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2} + \frac{4}{n^2\pi^2} (\cos \frac{n\pi}{2} - 1)
$$
Evaluate second integral ($1$ to $2$):
$$
\left[ \frac{2}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \right]_1^2 = \frac{2}{n\pi} (\sin n\pi - \sin \frac{n\pi}{2}) = -\frac{2}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2}
$$
Summing these and multiplying by $1/2$:
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \frac{2}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2} + \frac{4}{n^2\pi^2} (\cos \frac{n\pi}{2} - 1) - \frac{2}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2} \right]
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{2}{n^2\pi^2} \left( \cos \frac{n\pi}{2} - 1 \right)
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \int_{0}^{1} x \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx + \int_{1}^{2} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx \right]
$$
Integration by parts for first term:
$$
\int x \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} dx = -\frac{2x}{n\pi} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} + \frac{4}{n^2\pi^2} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2}
$$
Evaluate limits ($0$ to $1$):
$$
-\frac{2}{n\pi} \cos \frac{n\pi}{2} + \frac{4}{n^2\pi^2} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2}
$$
Second term limits ($1$ to $2$):
$$
\left[ -\frac{2}{n\pi} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \right]_1^2 = -\frac{2}{n\pi} (\cos n\pi - \cos \frac{n\pi}{2})
$$
Summing and multiplying by $1/2$:
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{2} \left[ -\frac{2}{n\pi} \cos \frac{n\pi}{2} + \frac{4}{n^2\pi^2} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2} - \frac{2}{n\pi} (-1)^n + \frac{2}{n\pi} \cos \frac{n\pi}{2} \right]
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{2}{n^2\pi^2} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2} - \frac{(-1)^n}{n\pi}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{3}{8} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{2}{n^2\pi^2} (\cos \frac{n\pi}{2} - 1) \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} + \left( \frac{2}{n^2\pi^2} \sin \frac{n\pi}{2} - \frac{(-1)^n}{n\pi} \right) \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \right]
$$

**13. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-5, 5)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
1, & -5 < x < 0 \\
1+x, & 0 \le x < 5 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = 5$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{5} \left[ \int_{-5}^{0} 1 \, dx + \int_{0}^{5} (1+x) \, dx \right]
$$
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{5} \left[ 5 + (5 + \frac{25}{2}) \right] = \frac{1}{5} \left( \frac{45}{2} \right) = \frac{9}{2}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{5} \left[ \int_{-5}^{0} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{5} \, dx + \int_{0}^{5} (1+x) \cos \frac{n\pi x}{5} \, dx \right]
$$
Combine the constant terms $1 \cdot \cos$:
$$
\int_{-5}^{5} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{5} \, dx = 0 \quad (\text{full period})
$$
So we just evaluate:
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{5} \int_{0}^{5} x \cos \frac{n\pi x}{5} \, dx
$$
By parts ($u=x, dv=\cos \dots$):
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{5} \left[ \frac{5x}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{5} + \frac{25}{n^2\pi^2} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{5} \right]_0^5
$$
Sine term at 5 is $\sin n\pi = 0$. Sine term at 0 is 0.
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{5} \left[ \frac{25}{n^2\pi^2} (\cos n\pi - 1) \right] = \frac{5}{n^2\pi^2} ((-1)^n - 1)
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{5} \left[ \int_{-5}^{0} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{5} \, dx + \int_{0}^{5} (1+x) \sin \frac{n\pi x}{5} \, dx \right]
$$
Combine constant terms $1 \cdot \sin$:
$$
\int_{-5}^{5} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{5} \, dx = 0
$$
Remaining term:
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{5} \int_{0}^{5} x \sin \frac{n\pi x}{5} \, dx
$$
By parts:
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{5} \left[ -\frac{5x}{n\pi} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{5} + \frac{25}{n^2\pi^2} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{5} \right]_0^5
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{5} \left[ -\frac{25}{n\pi} \cos n\pi + 0 - (0 + 0) \right] = -\frac{5}{n\pi} (-1)^n = \frac{5}{n\pi} (-1)^{n+1}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{9}{4} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{5((-1)^n - 1)}{n^2\pi^2} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{5} + \frac{5(-1)^{n+1}}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{5} \right]
$$

**14. Find the Fourier series of $f(x)$ on the interval $(-2, 2)$.**

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 
2+x, & -2 < x < 0 \\
2, & 0 \le x < 2 
\end{cases}
$$

**Solution:**
Here $p = 2$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \int_{-2}^{0} (2+x) \, dx + \int_{0}^{2} 2 \, dx \right]
$$
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{2} \left( [2x + \frac{x^2}{2}]_{-2}^0 + 4 \right) = \frac{1}{2} ( (0 - (-4 + 2)) + 4 ) = \frac{1}{2} (2+4) = 3
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \int_{-2}^{0} (2+x) \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx + \int_{0}^{2} 2 \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx \right]
$$
The constant 2 integrates to 0 over the full period.
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2} \int_{-2}^{0} x \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx
$$
Let $x = -t$. Limits become $2$ to $0$. $dx = -dt$.
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{2} \int_{2}^{0} (-t) \cos \frac{-n\pi t}{2} (-dt) = -\frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{2} t \cos \frac{n\pi t}{2} dt
$$
This integral is similar to Q13 (using $p=2$).
$$
\int_{0}^{2} t \cos \frac{n\pi t}{2} dt = \frac{4}{n^2\pi^2} ((-1)^n - 1)
$$
So,
$$
a_n = -\frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{4}{n^2\pi^2} ((-1)^n - 1) \right) = \frac{2}{n^2\pi^2} (1 - (-1)^n)
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{2} \int_{-2}^{0} x \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \, dx
$$
(Constants vanished). Let $x = -t$.
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{2} \int_{2}^{0} (-t) \sin \frac{-n\pi t}{2} (-dt) = \frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{2} -t \sin \frac{n\pi t}{2} dt
$$
$$
b_n = -\frac{1}{2} \left[ -\frac{2t}{n\pi} \cos \frac{n\pi t}{2} \right]_0^2 \quad \text{(Sine part is 0)}
$$
$$
b_n = -\frac{1}{2} \left( -\frac{4}{n\pi} \cos n\pi \right) = \frac{2}{n\pi} (-1)^n
$$
*Correction*: Check calculation.
$\int_0^2 t \sin \frac{n\pi t}{2} dt = [-\frac{2t}{n\pi} \cos] + \frac{4}{n^2\pi^2} \sin$.
Boundary 2: $-\frac{4}{n\pi} (-1)^n$. Boundary 0: 0.
So $b_n = -\frac{1}{2} (-\frac{4}{n\pi} (-1)^n) = \frac{2}{n\pi} (-1)^n$.
However, in $f(x)$, $x$ is on $(-2, 0)$. Let's check logic.
$f(x) = 2$ (even) $+ x$ (odd on negative side).
The even part gives 0 for $b_n$.
The odd part (function $g(x) = x$ on $(-2, 0)$ and $0$ on $(0, 2)$).
Direct calculation: $\int_{-2}^0 x \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} dx$.
$= [-\frac{2x}{n\pi} \cos]_ {-2}^0 + \int_{-2}^0 \dots$
$= (0 - (-\frac{-4}{n\pi} \cos(-n\pi))) = -\frac{4}{n\pi} (-1)^n$.
Divide by $p=2$ gives $-\frac{2}{n\pi}(-1)^n$.
Wait, substitution $t=-x$ gave $+$.
$\sin(-A) = -\sin A$. Three negatives in $-t \sin(-..) (-dt)$. Yes, negative.
So $b_n = -\frac{2}{n\pi}(-1)^n = \frac{2}{n\pi}(-1)^{n+1}$.

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{3}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{2(1 - (-1)^n)}{n^2\pi^2} \cos \frac{n\pi x}{2} + \frac{2(-1)^{n+1}}{n\pi} \sin \frac{n\pi x}{2} \right]
$$

**15. Find the Fourier series of $f(x) = e^x$ on $(-\pi, \pi)$.**

**Solution:**
Here $p = \pi$.

**Step 1: Calculate $a_0$.**
$$
a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} e^x \, dx = \frac{1}{\pi} (e^{\pi} - e^{-\pi}) = \frac{2\sinh \pi}{\pi}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $a_n$.**
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} e^x \cos nx \, dx
$$
Using the standard integral formula $\int e^{ax} \cos bx \, dx = \frac{e^{ax}}{a^2+b^2}(a \cos bx + b \sin bx)$:
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \frac{e^x}{1+n^2} (\cos nx + n \sin nx) \right]_{-\pi}^{\pi}
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{1}{\pi(1+n^2)} [ e^{\pi}(\cos n\pi + 0) - e^{-\pi}(\cos(-n\pi) + 0) ]
$$
$$
a_n = \frac{(-1)^n}{\pi(1+n^2)} (e^{\pi} - e^{-\pi}) = \frac{2 \sinh \pi (-1)^n}{\pi(1+n^2)}
$$

**Step 3: Calculate $b_n$.**
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} e^x \sin nx \, dx
$$
Using $\int e^{ax} \sin bx \, dx = \frac{e^{ax}}{a^2+b^2}(a \sin bx - b \cos bx)$:
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ \frac{e^x}{1+n^2} (\sin nx - n \cos nx) \right]_{-\pi}^{\pi}
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{1}{\pi(1+n^2)} [ e^{\pi}(0 - n(-1)^n) - e^{-\pi}(0 - n(-1)^n) ]
$$
$$
b_n = \frac{-n(-1)^n}{\pi(1+n^2)} (e^{\pi} - e^{-\pi}) = \frac{2n \sinh \pi (-1)^{n+1}}{\pi(1+n^2)}
$$

**Final Series:**
$$
f(x) = \frac{\sinh \pi}{\pi} + \frac{2 \sinh \pi}{\pi} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{(-1)^n}{1+n^2} \cos nx + \frac{n(-1)^{n+1}}{1+n^2} \sin nx \right]
$$
